### Abstract

Despite Bangladesh achieving a national electrification rate exceeding 99 percent,
significant disparities in energy access quality, reliability, and affordability persist
across the country's 495 sub-districts (upazilas). This study constructs and applies a
Multidimensional Energy Poverty Index (MEPI) at the upazila level, capturing five
dimensions of energy deprivation — Availability, Reliability, Adequacy, Quality, and
Affordability — across 18 household-level indicators sourced from the Bangladesh Bureau
of Statistics (BBS), Bangladesh Power Development Board (BPDB), and World Bank databases.

Results reveal stark spatial heterogeneity. MEPI scores range from 0.18 in peri-urban
Savar (Dhaka) to 0.85 in remote Rowangchhari (Chittagong Hill Tracts), a 4.7-fold
difference within the same national electrification framework. Approximately 35 percent
of sampled upazilas fall in the severely energy-poor category (MEPI > 0.66). Spatial
autocorrelation analysis (Moran's I = 0.72) confirms that energy poverty clusters
geographically, with hotspots concentrated in the Chittagong Hill Tracts, southern
coastal belt, and char island regions of Rangpur. The Affordability dimension exhibits
the highest inter-upazila variation, while Reliability is the most uniformly deficient
dimension nationally.

The MEPI framework offers a replicable, data-driven tool for sub-national energy poverty
monitoring aligned with SDG 7 (Affordable and Clean Energy). Findings support targeted
policy interventions including off-grid solar expansion in hill tract and coastal regions,
clean cooking fuel subsidies in haor wetlands, and reformed energy tariff structures in
char island communities. This study contributes the finest spatial resolution MEPI
analysis published for Bangladesh and provides an open-source Python codebase for
replication and scaling to the full 495-upazila national dataset.

**Keywords:** energy poverty, MEPI, spatial analysis, Bangladesh, upazila, SDG 7,
multidimensional index, geographic hotspots, clean energy, rural electrification
